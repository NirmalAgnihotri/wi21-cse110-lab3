What is a Haiku?

A Haiku is a form of short poetry that originated in Japan. The traditional Haiku contains 3 phrases with a total of 17 on, which in English translates to the 5-7-5 rule denoting the number of syllables on each line. However, Kerouac believed this rule did not translate well into English and formed his own, less restrictive style, once famously saying One day I will find the right words, and they will be simple.

Fun Fact about C++

In C++, if we declare an array called arr, arr[2] and 2[arr] are actually both valid methods of accessing the element at index 2 in the array. This is because in compilation, arr[2] becomes *(arr + 2) and 2[arr] becomes *(2 + arr). Both are equivalent, but the traditional arr[2] is certainly nicer.
